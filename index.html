Data Protection Act (2018)

The DPA is an Act of Parliament of the United Kingdom which aims to protect an individual's personal data stored on computers or organised paper filing systems. It complements the European Union's GDPR and supersedes the Data Protection Act (1998)

Data Protection Principles

The DPA outlines strict rules called 'data protection principles' which everyone responsible for using personal data has to follow. They must make sure the user's information is:

  • used fairly, lawfully, and clearly
  • used for explicitly specified purposes
  • used in a way that is relevant and necessary
  • accurate and kept up to date (where required)
  • kept for no longer than necessary
  • handled in a way that ensures adequate security (this includes protection against unlawful or unauthorised processing, access, loss, destruction, or damage)

Information Covered by the Act:

  • biometrics (where used for identification)
  • ethnic background
  • genetics
  • health
  • political opinions
  • race
  • religious beliefs
  • sex life or orientation
  • trade union membership
